Visual Hierarchy

  • Luna Amazon
  • luna.amazon.com

This website applies the design principle of Visual Hierarchy because it has organized and prioritized content to guide users' eyes. We have the “Monopoly Plus” banner that is displayed at the top with a great size where the user can see it first. The call to action “Play the classic game” and “Learn More” are secondary but still easily noticeable and this helps to form the hierarchy. Below we have a grid layout that showcases various games available where their titles are clear and visuals are appealing, making it easy for users to find their favorite game. Also, every game's visual is accompanied by a title ensuring users can quickly identify and select their preferred games. Lastly, to say that this website also applies the design principle of Contrast that helps to differentiate between elements and to create a focal point.

PARC: Alignment

  • IBM
  • ibm.com

The alignment principle is applied here because we have the elements of the page, such as headlines, subheadings, and call-to-action buttons, aligned vertically. This helps to provide a clear visual path for the user and makes it easier to read. Also, we have the text blocks aligned to the left of the website, which helps to keep it organized and maintain visual harmony. The “Try for Free” and “Meet watsonx.ai” buttons are aligned next to each other, creating a sense of balance and making it easy for users to see their options. They guide the viewer's eye through the content, making it easier to navigate and understand.

PARC: Repetition

  • Visme
  • visme.co

This website applies the principle of repetition because it uses similar elements throughout a design. For example, the font in this website in the headline and subheading uses the same font and style, creating a clear and consistent message that is easy to read. Also, the colors gray, white, and blue are repeated, and this helps to have a cohesive look. Lastly, we have the icons that are very similar with the same color and the stars with the same shape and color too. Even though they are logos from different companies, the principle of repetition helps them stay similar and pleasing to the eye.
